Overview

The Problem

AI models are only as good as the data they're trained on. At Intuit, domain experts — tax professionals, financial advisors, small business specialists — hold the judgment that makes a model response accurate or not. The challenge was translating that judgment into a form models could use.

EITL is a human-in-the-loop evaluation and training ecosystem. Experts act as labelers and annotators, reviewing outputs, flagging issues, and generating high-quality datasets that feed directly into model training and evaluation pipelines. My work focused on designing the tools that make this process consistent, efficient, and scalable.

Role & Process

How I Worked

I designed expert-facing workflows from the ground up — starting with deep sessions with the domain experts themselves to understand how they reason, what slows them down, and where inconsistency crept into their labeling. This shaped every interaction pattern in the tooling.

My work covered the full annotation surface: task queue design, label selection interfaces, qualitative input forms, and evaluation review flows. I partnered closely with data science to ensure the outputs matched what the training pipeline needed, and with engineering to keep implementation feasible.
